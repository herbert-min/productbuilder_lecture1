Various Cuisine Categories

Our service recommends a variety of foods loved by Koreans, classified by category. Learn about the characteristics and representative menus of each category.

Korean Cuisine

Traditional Korean food is characterized by fermented foods and a culture of various side dishes. There is a wealth of nutritious and delicious menus such as Kimchi Jjigae, Doenjang Jjigae, Bibimbap, Bulgogi, and Samgyeopsal. Korean food is also known to be healthy as it contains a lot of vegetables and fermented foods.

  • Representative Menu: Kimchi Jjigae, Doenjang Jjigae, Bibimbap
  • Features: Fermented foods, various side dishes
  • Health Points: Rich in probiotics

Chinese Cuisine

Chinese cuisine has different characteristics for each region, and in Korea, Jajangmyeon, Jjamppong, and Tangsuyuk are particularly popular. It is characterized by wok cooking, which is cooked quickly over a high fire, and uses various spices to create a deep flavor.

  • Representative Menu: Jajangmyeon, Jjamppong, Tangsuyuk
  • Features: Wok cooking, various sauces
  • Health Points: Utilization of various vegetables

Japanese Cuisine

Japanese cuisine is characterized by fresh ingredients and delicate cooking methods. Sushi, ramen, tonkatsu, and udon are also very popular in Korea. You can feel a clean yet deep taste with a cooking method that brings out the original taste of the ingredients.

  • Representative Menu: Sushi, Ramen, Tonkatsu, Udon
  • Features: Fresh ingredients, clean taste
  • Health Points: Rich in seafood

Western Cuisine

Western cuisine includes a variety of menus such as steak, pasta, pizza, and hamburgers. It is characterized by a rich taste and a feeling of fullness, and is also popular as a special day dining menu. You can choose according to your taste with various sauces and cooking methods.

  • Representative Menu: Steak, Pasta, Pizza
  • Features: Rich taste, various sauces
  • Health Points: Rich in protein

Tips for Choosing a Dinner Menu

Here are some practical tips for a healthy and delicious dinner. Refer to the guide below to choose the perfect dinner menu for today.

Seasonal Foods for the Season

Food made with seasonal ingredients is highly nutritious and tastes good. In spring, we recommend spring vegetable dishes such as shepherd's purse and wild garlic, in summer, cool naengmyeon or kong-guksu, in autumn, gizzard shad or mushroom dishes, and in winter, warm stews.

Consider Nutritional Balance

A balanced meal of carbohydrates, protein, and fat is important. A healthier meal can be achieved by adding meat or fish and vegetables to rice or noodles.

Choose a Different Menu from Lunch

If you had Korean food for lunch, try a different type of food for dinner. Eating a variety of foods will provide you with more nutrients evenly.

Consider Cooking Time

On busy days, choose a menu that can be cooked quickly, and on leisurely days, try a hearty dish. Using meal kits or convenience foods is also a good way.
